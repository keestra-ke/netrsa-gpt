## CHAPTER 1: THE ORIGIN — WHERE THIS IDEA STARTED

This idea comes from something very real. Something I noticed personally while working around my neighbourhood in Nairobi. I kept seeing the same problem happening over and over.

People are always searching for vacant houses. Even friends.  
*"Have you heard about a vacant anywhere?"*  
And I'm like — no I haven't. So I tell them: you need to walk around the neighbourhood. This is ghetto. Semi-slum places. You have to walk.

Because these places, houses are built like story buildings — 2 floors, 3 floors, 4 floors, 5 floors like that. Each floor is divided into rooms. Room 1 to room 10. Mostly single rooms. Each one has a price.

| Room Type | Price Range (Ksh/month) |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Single Room | 3,000 – 4,000 |
| Bedsitter | 5,000 – 5,500 |
| One Bedroom | 8,000 – 10,000 |
| Two Bedroom | 12,000+ |

Different types of houses. Different landlords. Different prices. And completely different conditions in every place. Security is different. Navigation is different. Drainage is different. Water availability is different. Electricity availability is different. Data services and Wi-Fi — different. Access to roads, matatu stages, supermarkets, shops, hospitals, schools — all different.

Tenants with real problems are moving. Maybe from Githurai to Baba Dogo, or Madare, or Kasarani. Someone trying to move but has no idea how the place looks. They don't know anything. They are just guessing.

So I want to build something for these people. Something that removes the guesswork completely.

## CHAPTER 2: THE CORE IDEA — PLATFORM VISION & BUILD STRATEGY

What if all this information already existed before you even moved? What if every house, every building, every room was already mapped and visible?

So instead of walking blindly, you just open an app and already understand everything — vacant houses, prices, photos, rules, environment, and how life in that place actually works.

> **"You are already walking inside the estate before you physically go there."**

That is **Vacant Kenya**. Also known as **Keja Scan**.

### 2.1 What the Platform Really Is

Most people will think this is a housing app. It is not just a housing app. **It is the digital life of the Kenyan estate.** Housing is the entry point. But once someone moves in, everything else — their neighbours, their services, their money, their social world, their reputation — all of it lives inside this app.

**The house gets you in the door. The community, the services, the economy, and the daily utility of the app keep you there forever.**

### 2.2 Staged Build Approach

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Stage 1** | Website first — accessible from any browser, anywhere in the country. No app download required. Lowest barrier to entry. |
| **Stage 2** | Mobile application — light, fast, easily accessible across cities, suburbs, and surrounding estates. Designed to work even on low-end Android phones with limited data. |

### 2.3 Target Areas — Where It Starts

The platform starts in the densest rental areas of Nairobi where the problem is most acute:

- Githurai 44 and 45
- Baba Dogo
- Madare
- Kayole
- Kasarani
- Roysambu
- Kahawa West
- Pipeline
- Zimmerman
- Rongai

After establishing critical mass in Nairobi, the model expands to Mombasa, Kisumu, Eldoret, Nakuru — and eventually across East Africa.

## CHAPTER 3: THE APP EXPERIENCE — FEEL, FLOW & INTERFACE DESIGN

### 3.1 The Feel — Fluid Like Liquid

The app must feel fluid. Like liquid. When you open it, it flows. It shows you a welcome feeling. It tells you:

> **"You are in the right place."**

Then it moves. Smooth. Natural. Like a village walkthrough — it takes you from where you are, to the place you want to stay. Even if you have never been there. Even if you don't have transport. Even if you don't have time. But you have money. You open this app, and it takes you there.

### 3.2 Opening Screen — The Live Housing Feed

When you open the app, it does not start with a search bar. It starts with a **live housing feed** showing what is happening right now. This is the TikTok principle applied to housing — you are already seeing things before you even know what you are looking for.

- **Promoted listings** — pushed to top
- **Most viewed houses right now**
- **Trending places and estates**
- **Newly posted vacant rooms**
- **High-demand areas** where many people are searching
- **Scarcity signals** — '3 people viewing this room right now', 'Posted 4 minutes ago'
- **Live activity** — 'New room posted in Kasarani', 'Caretaker just confirmed a vacancy'

The feed uses an algorithm that learns what you care about. Your area, your budget, your room type preferences. Over time the feed becomes uniquely yours — like Spotify's Discover Weekly but for housing.

### 3.3 Navigation Flow

| Feed | Explore | Map View | Deep Search |
|------|---------|----------|-------------|
| Live housing activity | Browse by area or type | Real-time pins + prices | Filter: price, floor, utilities |

### 3.4 Map View — Real-Time Pins

You type an area — Baba Dogo, Githurai, Kasarani, Kayole, Madare — or just browse. The system switches into a live map view. This is the most powerful screen in the app.

**REAL-TIME MAP**: When a landlord posts a listing, it appears on the map immediately — pinned, price visible. Ksh 4,000. Ksh 5,500. Ksh 10,000. Everything live. GPS-verified. Real locations only. Taken rooms shown differently. Nothing fake.

The map is a **live vacancy radar**. Seeing pins appear and disappear in real time creates FOMO — people check it constantly. This is what transforms the app from a search tool into something people watch like a live feed.

## CHAPTER 4: WHAT EVERY LISTING CONTAINS

Every listing is detailed because real life in these places is not simple. A listing is not just a photo and a price — it is a full picture of how life works there. Before you move in, you already know everything.

### 4.1 Physical Structure

- Building name and plot name
- Exact GPS location
- Estate name where it is found
- Floor number — very important in multi-storey buildings
- Room type: single room, bedsitter, one-bedroom, two-bedroom
- Room size and dimensions — meters, rectangle or square (e.g. 5m x 4m)
- Furniture fit model — tenant can calculate if their furniture will fit in the room before moving

### 4.2 Visuals

- Room photos — taken on-site at exact time of posting, GPS-stamped
- Building photos
- Plot or building structure photos
- Surrounding environment photos

**Photos cannot be uploaded from gallery.** They must be taken live at the location. The system checks GPS at the moment the photo is taken.

### 4.3 Financial Info

- Rent amount
- Deposit required
- Garbage collection fee — often hidden, now mandatory to declare
- Everything included — no hidden costs
- Payment method: M-Pesa or bank payment instructions
- Payment structure and schedule

#### Early Payment Discount System

Landlords can set an early payment window inside the app. If the tenant pays before the deadline through the platform, a discount is automatically applied. Both sides benefit — the landlord gets early, certain cash flow, and the tenant saves money.

**EARLY RENT DISCOUNT**: Landlord sets: 'Pay before the 5th — save Ksh 100.' Tenant sees a countdown inside My Building: '3 days left to pay early and save Ksh 100.' They tap, pay via M-Pesa, discount applied automatically. After the deadline, full rent is required.

**Why this works for landlords:**
- Gets paid earlier every month — no more chasing on the 15th
- No awkward gate conversations — payment confirmed digitally
- Full payment record and history — disputes become impossible
- The Ksh 100 discount is worth less than the certainty and early cash flow

**Why this works for tenants:**
- Direct, immediate financial reward — actual money off rent, not just points
- On a Ksh 4,000 single room, Ksh 100 off is 2.5% — over 12 months that is Ksh 1,200 saved
- Countdown timer creates urgency — they open the app and pay on time
- Paying early through app also earns Community Score points

**Discount variations the landlord can choose to offer:**

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Early window discount | Pay before a set date (e.g. 5th of month) — save a fixed amount (e.g. Ksh 100) |
| 3-month advance discount | Pay 3 months in advance — landlord offers a small bonus (e.g. one week free) |
| Annual payment discount | Pay full year upfront — landlord offers up to one month free (landlord opts in) |
| Loyalty rent freeze | Consistent on-time payment for 6+ months — landlord can offer a rent freeze or deposit reduction |
| Referral rent credit | Tenant refers a neighbour who moves in — landlord credits Ksh 200–500 toward next rent |

The discount is always landlord-opted — they choose the amount and the window. The platform enforces and automates it. No manual calculation. No disputes. No confusion. If the tenant pays through cash after the early window, no discount applies and no record is created.

This feature, combined with **Deposit Portability**, makes paying rent through the app the financially rational choice every single month — not just once.

### 4.4 Ownership Info

- Landlord name
- Caretaker name and role
- Agency name if any
- Verified contacts

### 4.5 Deposit Escrow System

**ESCROW DEPOSIT**: A tenant makes a deposit inside the app. The money is held in a secure account. It is only released to the caretaker or landlord after the tenant has verified the place and confirmed the booking. No more losing deposits to fake listings.

**Flow**: Tenant views listing → contacts caretaker → agrees terms → deposits into escrow → visits or verifies → confirms → funds released. If something is wrong, dispute process protects the tenant. Cash offers zero protection. Escrow offers everything.

### 4.6 Deposit Portability — Carry Your Deposit When You Move

This is one of the most important and original features of the platform. It solves a problem that every Kenyan tenant faces at the worst possible moment — the moment they want to move.

**Right now, when a tenant decides to move, this is what happens:**
- They must chase the old landlord for the deposit back — sometimes for weeks, sometimes months
- They must come up with a fresh deposit for the new place before the old one returns
- Some tenants never get their deposit back at all
- Some cannot move even when they want to, because they cannot afford two deposits at once

**DEPOSIT PORTABILITY**: Because Vacant Kenya holds deposits in escrow, the deposit never needs to leave the system. When a tenant moves, their deposit travels with them — from one building to the next. No chasing. No waiting. No losing money. The deposit moves like luggage.

**This is how it works:**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Step 1 — Decision** | Tenant decides to move. They find a new listing on the platform. |
| **Step 2 — Check Match** | System checks if the new landlord's required deposit matches or is close to the existing escrow balance. |
| **Step 3 — Request** | Tenant initiates a Deposit Transfer request inside the app. |
| **Step 4 — Old Release** | Old landlord or caretaker confirms the move-out and releases the deposit from the escrow. They cannot withhold it without a documented, verified reason. |
| **Step 5 — Transfer** | The deposit moves directly from the old escrow to the new escrow — without ever going to the tenant's M-Pesa account. It stays inside the platform. |
| **Step 6 — New Place** | New landlord receives the deposit as escrow. Tenant walks into the new place already financially settled. |

**Handling the difference:**
- If the new deposit is the same amount — zero extra cost to the tenant. Full transfer.
- If the new deposit is higher — tenant tops up only the difference, not the full amount.
- If the new deposit is lower — the surplus is returned to the tenant via M-Pesa immediately.

**If the old landlord refuses to release the deposit without a valid reason:**
- The platform has timestamps, records, caretaker confirmations, and the full tenancy history
- Dispute resolution process is triggered
- The landlord's verified profile is flagged — affecting their listing visibility
- Cash deposits have zero of this protection

> **WHY THIS IS TRANSFORMATIONAL**: Most Kenyan tenants are trapped in houses they want to leave because they cannot afford two deposits at once. Deposit Portability removes this trap entirely. Moving becomes financially painless for the first time. This feature alone could make Vacant Kenya the default choice for every renter in Nairobi.

This is also a powerful incentive for tenants to always pay their deposit through the platform in the first place — because cash deposits cannot be ported. Only in-app escrow deposits can travel with you.

### 4.7 Reviews and Ratings

- Safety ratings from verified tenants
- Water and electricity reliability reviews
- General estate reviews
- Reviews from people who visited or toured the property
- Rent price history — exposes landlords who raise rent excessively

**Example**: 'Bedsitter in Kahawa West — 2022: Ksh 6,500 | 2023: Ksh 7,000 | 2024: Ksh 9,000. Rent increased 38% in 2 years.' This creates transparency and social pressure that keeps landlords honest.

### 4.8 GPS Auto-Distance Estimation

When institutions (schools, hospitals, mosques, churches, police stations) have registered and verified their location on the platform, the system automatically estimates and displays distances on every listing:

- Distance to nearest hospital or clinic
- Distance to nearest primary and secondary school
- Distance to nearest mosque and church
- Distance to matatu stages and major roads
- Distance to nearest police station

No manual entry needed from the landlord. The information appears automatically once institutions are registered.

### 4.9 Contact Before Booking

A tenant can contact the landlord or caretaker directly before committing. Before booking. Before paying anything. You talk first. You decide with full information. No pressure.

## CHAPTER 5: PROPERTY RULES LAYER

Every building has its own culture. Caretakers and landlords document these rules so tenants know exactly what they are entering — before they move in. No surprises on day one.

- Gate closing time and locking rules
- Night access rules
- Visitor rules — when, how many, overnight policy
- Quiet hours
- Cleanliness standards for shared spaces — corridors, toilets, taps
- Social life rules — where football and movies can be watched
- Gathering rules — where and when gatherings are allowed
- Noise control restrictions
- General behaviour and discipline expectations
- Substance and disturbance rules

### 5.1 Caretaker Document Sharing

**DOCUMENT SHARING**: The caretaker or landlord shares documents and notices directly with verified tenants of their building only. Rent notices, utility updates, rule changes, community information — all delivered in-app to exactly the right people.

- Rent notices and payment reminders
- Utility schedule updates
- Community notices and building announcements
- Schools and churches can also post their memos and events within the same building channel
- Private — information stays within the verified building community

### 5.2 Caretaker Credits for Tenant Registration

Caretakers earn credits every time they register and verify a tenant into their building. More tenants registered = more credits = more platform benefits. This keeps the building data live and accurate and gives caretakers a real financial reason to stay active.

Each building becomes a structured small community. And these small communities — in the same estate — also form a larger community together, with shared alerts, team building activities, churches, mosques, and neighbourhood coordination.

## CHAPTER 7: TENANT SYSTEM — LINKING PEOPLE TO PLACES

### 7.1 Link My Residence

If you already live somewhere, you can link your current home to your profile. This is how the city gets mapped — through real people who actually live there.

- Search for your building in the system
- Link it to your profile
- The caretaker verifies you as a real tenant via SMS confirmation
- You become part of that building's digital community

**TENANT CREDITS REWARD**: When a tenant verifies their location and is confirmed by the caretaker, they earn free credits. This incentive makes tenants want to verify. The caretaker benefits. The platform stays accurate. Everyone wins.

If the building is not yet listed, you request the caretaker or landlord to list it, then connect.

### 7.2 Move-Out Self-Reporting

When a tenant is moving out, they open the app and tap 'Moving Out'. They register where they are going next. This single feature solves a major operational problem for caretakers.

- Tenant self-reports move-out from within the app
- Registers destination — where they are moving to
- Room is automatically marked as 'Coming Soon Vacant' — allowing early viewings
- Serious enquirers can book early viewings
- Tenant earns 'Smooth Exit Points' for keeping room clean during viewings
- Caretaker no longer needs to post a separate vacancy ad — saves money
- Room becomes fully vacant in real time on the day of departure

This keeps the platform live without extra work from the caretaker. And it creates a chain reaction — someone moving out in Baba Dogo sends an instant alert to people searching in Baba Dogo.

### 7.3 Tenant Good Tenant Score

When a tenant moves out, the caretaker can rate them on: cleanliness, payment reliability, noise level, and general behaviour. This score follows the tenant to their next house.

- **Score 85+** — Priority viewing slots, sometimes lower deposit
- **Score 90+** — 'Good Tenant' badge, shown first in landlord searches
- **Score below 60** — Needs extra caretaker approval before moving into next place

This creates real incentive for tenants to behave well — not because they are forced to, but because their score is worth something when they want to move to a better place.

### 7.4 Tenant Skills and Services Profile

A tenant's profile can include the services they are personally qualified to offer. A verified tenant who is a plumber, electrician, fundi, or mechanic can list their skills and get verified work through the platform.

**TENANT AS SERVICE PROVIDER**: Any verified tenant can add services to their profile. Once they receive a job and payment is confirmed through the platform, that job appears on their public record. Reviews and ratings build their reputation. High ratings = more work. Badges awarded for milestones.

- Tenant adds skills: plumber, electrician, fundi, painter, mechanic, etc.
- Jobs must be paid through the platform to count as verified work
- Every verified job adds to the tenant's work history
- Ratings and review comments left by customers
- Badges awarded for high ratings and volume of work
- Higher ratings = higher visibility in search results
- This motivates excellent work — one bad review affects future bookings

## CHAPTER 8: MY BUILDING DASHBOARD

The Daily Home Screen After You Move In

Once a tenant links their residence, the app opens directly to their building feed — not the general feed. This is the most important retention feature in the entire platform. It makes the app feel like it belongs to you personally.

> **"This is not a housing app anymore. This is your estate, digitised."**

### 8.1 Building Pulse — The Daily Status Screen

The Building Pulse is the first thing a tenant sees every morning. It answers the only questions that matter right now:

| Status | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Water Status** | Constant / Low / Off — updated by caretaker |
| **Electricity** | Stable / Blackout / Token needed / Generator on |
| **Security Level** | Normal / Alert / Incident reported |
| **Garbage Collection** | Time of collection today |
| **Caretaker on Duty** | Name + M-Pesa number for quick payment |
| **Quick Poll** | 'Should we fix the gate light this week?' — Yes/No |
| **Today's Notices** | Any caretaker announcements for today |

People will open the app every morning just to check if water is running before they shower. That is a daily habit formed without any marketing.

### 8.2 Full My Building Dashboard Structure

1. **Building Pulse** — Water, electricity, security, garbage — live daily status
2. **Community Score Card** — Your score (87/100), rank in building, trend this week
3. **Quick Actions** — Report issue, post job, refer friend, pay rent
4. **Leaderboard Carousel** — Your rank — Overall, Job Connector, Building Keeper, Smooth Neighbour
5. **Neighbour Exchange** — Buy, sell, borrow within the building — hyper-local marketplace
6. **Inner Jobs** — Jobs posted within the building and estate, verified with badges
7. **Chama Balance** — Community savings circle — contribution and balance
8. **Caretaker Notices** — Documents and notices from caretaker — for verified tenants only
9. **Points Balance Widget** — Your current points — 'Redeem for airtime or mama fua'
10. **Move-Out Section** — If moving, manage your transition, earn smooth exit points

### 8.3 Neighbour Exchange — Hyper-Local Marketplace Inside the Building

Inside My Building there is a free swap/sell section. Because these are verified neighbours in the same building, trust is the highest it can be anywhere on the platform.

- Selling my old mattress — Ksh 1,500, same floor
- Need someone to take over my Wi-Fi subscription this month
- Borrowing sufuria for a party tonight
- Selling a 2-burner gas cooker — Ksh 2,500, pickup in Baba Dogo Phase 3

People moving houses often sell items cheaply. People who just moved in need those items urgently. The Neighbour Exchange connects them instantly, within the same building.

### 8.4 Community Wallet — Chama Inside the Building

Every building can have its own mini-chama. This is one of the deepest retention hooks in the platform because money creates mandatory engagement.

- Tenants contribute Ksh 100 or Ksh 200 monthly
- Money stays inside the building group, managed transparently through M-Pesa
- Can be used for: emergency repairs (fixing shared toilet, painting corridor), group buying (cheaper Wi-Fi for the whole floor), helping a tenant who lost their job
- App shows transparent balance — everyone sees what is in, what went out
- Loan rotation — members can borrow small amounts with auto-repayment rules

People will check the app weekly to see their chama balance. Money is never forgotten.

### 8.5 Building Vacancy Bounty — Tenants as Micro-Agents

When a room in your own building becomes vacant, the system notifies only people in that same building first — 24 hours before it goes public.

- First tenant who refers a serious friend who actually moves in earns: Ksh 500–1,000 airtime or M-Pesa, or referral credit towards next month's rent (landlord can opt in)
- This turns every tenant into a micro-agent for their own building
- They keep the app open because they are literally earning from the people around them

## CHAPTER 9: INNER JOBS — THE ESTATE JOB BOARD

Inner Jobs is the estate's own trusted job board. It lives inside My Building and serves the most important need of low-income urban Kenyans — finding local work without having to travel far. A job in your compound. An apprenticeship at the Naivas on the ground floor. A cleaning role at the church down the road.

Every Inner Job has a clear trust badge so tenants know exactly what they are looking at.

### 9.1 Three Trust Levels for Every Job Post

| Level | Badge | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Level 1 — Tenant Verified** | 🟢 Green Tick | Posted by someone linked to a room in the building. Shows: 'John – Room 12, 3rd Floor' + their Good Tenant Score (92/100). Trust from physical familiarity. |
| **Level 2 — Caretaker Approved** | 🔵 Blue Shield | Before the post goes live to the whole building or estate, the caretaker gets a push notification to approve. Once approved: blue shield + 'Approved by Caretaker Mama Akinyi'. Stops fake or suspicious posts instantly. |
| **Level 3 — Institution Verified** | 🏆 Gold Badge | For schools, supermarkets, churches, clinics, salons, cybercafes registered in the estate. Posts go live instantly with their logo. 'Official Job — Gold Verified'. |

### 9.2 Institution Verification — How Gold Badge Works

Institutions can get verified in one afternoon. Dead simple for a head teacher, branch manager, pastor, or salon owner.

- Institution taps 'Register as Institution' — separate from tenant/landlord flow
- Chooses type: Supermarket, School, Church, Clinic, Salon, Cyber, Other
- Enters: full official name, exact location pin, official contact, short description
- Uploads any one proof: photo of signboard, business registration, school letter, church certificate, KRA PIN
- System notifies nearest verified caretaker + 3 nearby tenants: 'Do you recognise this as the real Naivas / St. Mary's? Yes/No'
- If caretaker says Yes + at least 2 tenants confirm → Gold Badge activated instantly
- Gold badge never expires as long as posts are responsible
- Every 6 months: one-tap fresh photo of signboard for maintenance

### 9.3 Safety Layers for Inner Jobs

- All posts time-limited — expire after 14 days unless renewed
- After job is filled, both poster and hired person confirm 'Job Taken' inside app — both get +5 Community Points
- Anonymous reporting button on every post: 'This looks fake' → 3 reports hides post pending review
- App blocks suspicious language automatically — 'send 500 first' triggers review
- Chat inside app monitored lightly for scam patterns
- Good Tenant Score below 60 requires extra caretaker approval to post

### 9.4 How Inner Jobs Keeps People in the App Daily

Morning push: '2 new Inner Jobs in your building — one from Naivas, one apprenticeship at the mechanic.' A young guy in Baba Dogo wakes up, opens My Building, sees a mechanic apprenticeship at the compound garage, and applies before leaving for the day. A mother sees the school needs a cleaner and sends her sister's details.

When a tenant posts a job for a neighbour and it gets filled, they get a celebration: 'You helped someone eat today' + Community Points. Institutions keep posting because they reach exactly the right people — local residents who don't need to travel far. Everyone stays in the app.

## CHAPTER 10: LANDLORD & CARETAKER SIDE — VERIFICATION, ANTI-FRAUD & WORKSPACE

### 10.1 Registration Fee — The First Barrier Against Fraud

**REGISTRATION FEE**: To register as a landlord or caretaker, a non-refundable fee is paid via M-Pesa — Ksh 500. This deters bad actors. If someone tries to register as a fake landlord, they lose their money. Real landlords will not hesitate to pay. This is not about revenue — it is about filtering out fraudsters before they enter the system.

### 10.2 Verification Before Posting — Anti-Fraud System

**ANTI-FRAUD**: You cannot fake it. You must be physically inside the premises to post. The system verifies your GPS location at the time of photo upload. Everything is accountable. No old photos. No stock images. No fabrication.

- Verify GPS location — must be physically on-site when posting
- Verify phone number
- Verify identity — national ID number required
- Photos taken at that exact time and location — GPS-stamped, cannot be from gallery

### 10.3 Landlord Verification Layers — Real from Fake

How do you know if someone posting a property is a genuine landlord, a legitimate caretaker, or a fraudster? The system uses layered verification — not just one check. Multiple layers combined give very high confidence.

| Verification Layer | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| **Title Deed** | Primary proof of ownership. Strongest verification. Cross-checked with land registry. |
| **Electricity Account** | KPLC meter or account registered to the owner. Bills show account name and physical address. |
| **Water Account** | Utility account registered to property owner — shows address and name. |
| **National ID + KRA PIN** | Linked to real identity. Cross-referenced with ownership documents. |
| **Business Permit** | Required if it is an agency or property management company. |
| **Caretaker Letter** | Signed and witnessed letter from the registered landlord authorising the caretaker to list on their behalf. |
| **GPS + On-site Photo** | Must be physically inside the property when posting. GPS of photo compared to registered GPS of property. |
| **M-Pesa Registration** | Ksh 500 entry fee — filters out fake registrations before they start. |

A landlord with title deed + electricity account + GPS-verified on-site photos = very high confidence. A caretaker needs the landlord's authorisation letter + GPS verification + tenant community confirmation.

### 10.4 Caretaker Verification Process

Caretakers are the heartbeat of every estate. They are verified properly so tenants trust the Building Pulse, job approvals, and ratings they produce.

- Landlord nominates the caretaker when adding the property
- Caretaker downloads app, chooses 'I am Caretaker for [Building]', enters: full name, ID number, phone number, exact building on map, landlord's contact
- Landlord gets push notification to confirm — mandatory step, no fake caretakers
- First 5 tenants already linked to the building get quiet notification: 'Is this Mama Akinyi the real caretaker? Yes/No'
- 3 or more tenants confirm → Caretaker gets Verified Caretaker Badge (blue shield + name)
- Caretaker must update Building Pulse at least 3 times per week — badge turns yellow after 14 days of inactivity
- Every 6 months: quick re-confirmation by landlord + 2 tenants

### 10.5 Powers of a Verified Caretaker

- Approve or reject Inner Job posts in their building
- Mark rooms as vacant when tenant moves out
- Rate outgoing tenants — feeds into Community Score
- Update utility schedules, rules, and notices
- Share documents to specific building tenants only
- Earn credits redeemable for airtime, tools, or reduced posting fees

### 10.6 Mandatory Listing Fields

When posting, nothing is optional. Everything is mandatory. Fraud is controlled through strictness.

- Room dimensions — meters, shape
- Floor level and room type
- All utility conditions
- Property rules
- Garbage collection fee
- GPS-verified on-site photos
- Caretaker and landlord details with ID numbers

## CHAPTER 16: LEADERBOARDS — HEALTHY COMPETITION THAT KEEPS PEOPLE OPENING DAILY

Leaderboards turn good behaviour into friendly competition — like Sunday evening football banter in Githurai or Baba Dogo. When your name is on the board and your neighbours can see it, that hits different. Four parallel leaderboards run at once so different types of people can win.

### 16.1 Four Leaderboard Categories

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Overall Community Score** | Pure total points this week/month. The main board. |
| **Job Connector** | Points earned from posting/filling Inner Jobs and helping with apprenticeships. |
| **Building Keeper** | Points from updating Building Pulse, reporting issues, verifying institutions, paying rent on time, Chama contributions. Rewards the quiet heroes. |
| **Smooth Neighbour** | Points from Neighbour Exchange sales/lending, successful referrals, and helping during move-outs. |

A shy tenant who always reports water problems can top the Building Keeper board even if their overall score is average. Everyone has a chance to be seen.

### 16.2 Four Levels of Competition

- **Level 1: My Building** — Top 10 tenants in your exact building. Resets every Monday.
- **Level 2: Estate** — Top 20 across all buildings in the estate (e.g. Githurai 45).
- **Level 3: Zone / Area** — All of Githurai or all of Kasarani.
- **Level 4: All-Kenya Top Community Heroes** — Monthly only, once at scale.

### 16.3 Weekly & Monthly Rewards

**Weekly**: Top 3 in each category inside the building get 200 bonus points + 'Weekly Hero' badge on profile for 7 days.  
**Monthly Hall of Fame**: Top 5 Overall + Top 5 in each special category across the estate get 800–1,500 bonus points, featured in main app feed, and all redemptions worth 20% more for the entire next month.

### 16.4 Caretaker Leaderboard

All verified caretakers in the estate are ranked by: speed of approving jobs, accuracy and frequency of Building Pulse updates, tenant satisfaction ratings. Top caretaker gets 'Super Caretaker' badge + small bonus from participating landlords. This motivates caretakers to stay active — their reputation is public.

**Safety**: bottom ranks are hidden (only you see your own rank if low). No public shaming. Anonymous mode available: 'Show as Room 12 only'. Friendly push: 'You are only 45 points behind Mama Akinyi for #1 Building Keeper — post that Inner Job today!'
